Tiramisu

Heavenly Tiramisu: A Layered Delight

Experience the bliss of our Tiramisu, a classic Italian dessert that feels like a warm hug in every bite. It's made with delicate layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers and creamy mascarpone cheese, finished with a dusting of cocoa powder. Each spoonful is a perfect blend of rich flavors and smooth textures, creating a treat that's both indulgent and comforting. Whether you're sharing it with loved ones or enjoying it on your own, this Tiramisu promises a little slice of happiness in every serving.

Ingredients

  • Ladyfingers (Savoiardi) - The classic choice for soaking in coffee.
  • Mascarpone Cheese - Rich and creamy, essential for the filling.
  • Coffee - Strong brewed coffee or espresso for soaking the ladyfingers.
  • Sugar - To sweeten the mascarpone mixture.
  • Eggs - Yolks for the creamy filling, and whites if you want to make it lighter.
  • Cocoa Powder - For dusting on top to add a touch of chocolate flavor.
  • Vanilla Extract - For a hint of warmth in the cream.

How To Make Tiramisu

  1. Prepare Coffee Mixture: Mix the cooled coffee with 2 tablespoons of sugar. Set aside.
  2. Make the Mascarpone Cream: In a bowl, beat the egg yolks with 1 tablespoon of sugar until creamy and smooth.Add the mascarpone cheese and vanilla extract to the yolk mixture. Mix until well combined.
  3. Whip Egg Whites: In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the whipped egg whites into the mascarpone mixture until well combined. Be careful not to deflate the mixture.
  4. Assemble the Tiramisu: Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture, making sure not to soak them too long. Arrange a layer of dipped ladyfingers in the bottom of a serving dish. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers. Add another layer of dipped ladyfingers on top. Spread the remaining mascarpone mixture over the second layer of ladyfingers.
  5. Chill and Serve: Cover and refrigerate the Tiramisu for at least 4 hours, or overnight for best results. Before serving, dust the top with cocoa powder.
